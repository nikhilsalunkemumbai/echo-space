# ECHO — Design Constitution

*Version 1.0 — Ratified at project founding*

---

## Preamble

This constitution governs all design decisions for the Volatility Echo Rehearsal System. It exists because good intentions are not sufficient protection against feature creep, commercial pressure, or the gradual erosion of a project's purpose.

Every article blocks an entire class of feature requests. Any proposed change that violates an article requires a formal amendment process, not a workaround.

---

## Article I — Rehearsal Space, Not Arena

**Principle:** ECHO is a space to practice, not a competition to win.

**What it blocks:**
- Leaderboards of any kind
- Win/lose states
- Scores, ratings, or performance grades
- Comparative metrics between players
- Achievements or badges for specific outcomes

**Why:** The moment players compete, they optimize. Optimization destroys the learning signal. A community cannot be optimized. The steward who "wins" has misunderstood the system.

---

## Article II — Partial Opacity Over Full Transparency

**Principle:** The simulation knows everything. The player sees almost nothing numerical.

**What it blocks:**
- Display of volatility (V) as a number
- Display of crisis probability as a percentage
- Display of cohesion (C) as a number
- Display of agent engagement scores as numbers
- Any metric that enables optimization of the hidden state

**What is permitted:**
- Categorical mood states (Stable / Wavering / Tense)
- Narrative hints after crisis events
- The Community Chronicle as a qualitative end-state summary

**Why:** Showing numbers invites optimization. Optimization turns rehearsal into a puzzle. Puzzles can be solved. Communities cannot.

---

## Article III — Invitation Over Evaluation

**Principle:** The tone is "Would you like to sit with us for a while?" — not a test, not a challenge.

**What it blocks:**
- Tutorial pressure or forced onboarding sequences
- Performance grading or feedback scores
- Evaluative language ("You did well", "You failed")
- Any framing that implies there is a correct way to play

**What is required:**
- Onboarding language that equalises status between system and player
- The Imperfection Prompt: an early reassurance that there are no perfect runs
- Chronicle language that describes what happened, not how well the player performed

**Why:** If players feel evaluated, they perform rather than practice. Performance and rehearsal are incompatible.

---

## Article IV — The Holding of Tensions

**Principle:** ECHO is governed by active, ongoing management of five permanent design tensions. Its health is measured by its capacity to sustain these tensions without collapsing to one pole.

| Tension | Poles to resist |
|---------|----------------|
| Rehearsal vs Contemplation | Not just a reflective essay; not a training regime |
| Opacity vs Affordance | Not mystification; not a spreadsheet |
| Non-prescription vs Earned Asymmetry | Not moral abdication; not preaching |
| Preservation vs Evolution | Not a museum; not a product that drifts |
| Quiet vs Friction | Not one temperament's game; not sensory overload |

**Why:** Any feature that resolves a tension permanently has misunderstood the system. The tensions are the system.

---

## Amendment Process

Any proposed change to a locked parameter, a constitutional article, or a core mechanic requires:

1. A written case for which article it strengthens or reinterprets
2. A falsifiable hypothesis for how it deepens the rehearsal
3. A test scope with a defined rollback clause

Proposed changes that cannot meet all three criteria are rejected. This is not bureaucracy. This is design hygiene.

---

## The hardest rule

> *The artifact does not solve. It rehearses. The moment you let it claim more, it becomes dishonest.*
